# Canonical Audience Personas

This document defines the standardized audience personas used in the UC OSPO Education lesson inventory.

## Final Persona List

The following 10 canonical personas represent the primary audiences for open source education content:

1. **Beginners** - Newcomers to open source with little to no prior experience
2. **PhD Students** - Graduate students working on research and dissertations
3. **Postdocs** - Postdoctoral researchers in academic settings
4. **Researchers** - General research practitioners across disciplines
5. **Research Software Engineers (RSEs)** - Specialists who write code for research
6. **Software Developers** - Professional software engineers and developers
7. **Data Scientists** - Practitioners focused on data analysis and modeling
8. **Community Managers** - Those who build and maintain open source communities
9. **Maintainers** - Project maintainers and core contributors
10. **Faculty** - Academic faculty, PIs, and grant-seeking researchers

## Usage Guidelines

- Lessons can target multiple personas (comma-separated in Google Sheet)
- Use exact persona names as listed above
- Order personas from most to least relevant for each lesson
- Avoid creating new persona variations
